Travel Locations

  • Istanbul, Turkey: A city that straddles Europe and Asia, known for its rich history and cultural landmarks like Hagia Sophia, a masterpiece blending Byzantine and Ottoman architecture; the Blue Mosque with its iconic minarets and blue tiles; Topkapi Palace, home to Ottoman sultans for centuries; the bustling Grand Bazaar, one of the world’s oldest markets; and the Bosphorus Cruise, offering scenic views of palaces, bridges, and waterfront mansions.
    Read more
  • Athens, Greece: The cradle of Western civilization, known for its ancient landmarks such as the Acropolis, Parthenon, and the Temple of Olympian Zeus; the historic Plaka neighborhood with its narrow streets and neoclassical architecture; and the National Archaeological Museum, housing some of the most important artifacts from a variety of archaeological locations around Greece.
    Read more
  • Vienna, Austria: A city renowned for its artistic and intellectual legacy, with landmarks like the Schönbrunn Palace, the Hofburg, and St. Stephen's Cathedral; the Ringstrasse boulevard lined with grand buildings, monuments, and parks; and the Vienna State Opera, one of the leading opera houses in the world.
    Read more
  • Tirana, Albania: The vibrant capital city known for its colorful Ottoman, Fascist, and Soviet-era architecture; Skanderbeg Square, the heart of the city; the National History Museum with exhibits spanning Albania's history; and the Dajti Ekspres cable car offering panoramic views of the city.
    Read more
  • Rome, Italy: The Eternal City, famous for its nearly 3,000 years of globally influential art, architecture, and culture; landmarks such as the Colosseum, the Roman Forum, and the Pantheon; Vatican City, home to St. Peter's Basilica and the Vatican Museums; and the Trevi Fountain, a baroque masterpiece.
    Read more
  • Paris, France: The City of Light, known for its art, fashion, and culture; iconic landmarks like the Eiffel Tower, Notre-Dame Cathedral, and the Louvre Museum; the charming Montmartre district; and the Seine River cruises offering views of the city's historic architecture.
    Read more
  • Stockholm, Sweden: The capital city spread across 14 islands, known for its beautiful archipelago, the medieval old town of Gamla Stan, the Royal Palace, and the Vasa Museum, which houses a well-preserved 17th-century warship.
    Read more
  • Reykjavik, Iceland: The northernmost capital of the world, known for its modernist architecture, the Hallgrímskirkja church, the Harpa concert hall, and the nearby Blue Lagoon geothermal spa; as well as its vibrant arts scene and nightlife.
